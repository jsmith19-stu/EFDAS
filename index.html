Services

The Early Fire Detection and Alert System ( E-FDAS) comprises of a standalone renewable energy powered module that equipped with smoke detection sensors, a digital anemometer, a GSM module, that will form the backbone of the system.

Working in conjunction with a database that logs all the relevant information about the alerts, local authorities will be able to detect trends that are beginning to develop. The main aim of this system is to provide proactive monitoring, and alert system that enables the relevant authorities, such as the fire services, forestry department, or the Rural Agricultural Development Agency (RADA) to be on the front foot against fires. The system will provide the authorities with important data and information that can aid in properly responding, planning and if necessary awareness campaigns.



Wild Fire Safety Tips

Over the last 10 years, we have experienced several extreme bushfire seasons and major bushfires threatened properties valued in the millions .Despite bushfires being a common hazard in the Jamaican summer months, property owners remain unprepared to mitigate the risk of damage to their property and farm lands.If you have a property in a bushfire prone area, here are 6 tips you can use to reduce your bushfire risk:

  • Tip # 1

    Prepare a bushfire survival plan

    The Plan should outline the steps you will need to take to ensure safety for yourself and property. Remember to consider different situations – bushfire conditions change rapidly. Don’t just have a Plan A, also consider Plans B, C and D

  • Tip # 2

    Understand bushfire danger ratings

    Ensure you are familiar with the meaning of different Bushfire Alerts levels and Fire Danger Ratings. This will help you determine when to trigger your Bushfire Survival Plan.

  • Tip # 3

    Clean-up outside

    Keep outdoor combustible materials to a minimum. Ensure dry foliage such as fallen trees and other debris are cleared away, particularly from open lands and around the property . Trees overhanging buildings should be cut back where permitted.

  • Tip # 4

    Help firefighters with water supply

    Swimming pools, water tanks and dams may serve as a vital water supply for firefighters. If you have bulk water storage, display a Static Water Supply (SWS) sign at the property entrance.

  • Tip # 5

    Be Ember Safe

    Embers can travel kilometres ahead of the fire front and are the leading cause of property fires during a bushfire.

  • Tip # 6

    Leave early

    Swimming pools, water tanks and dams may serve as a vital water supply for firefighters. If you have bulk water storage, display a Static Water Supply (SWS) sign at the property entrance.

  • Tip # 7

    Preparation is vital

    Choosing to stay and defend may place you at serious risk. Not all properties can be defended. If you choose to stay, make sure you are well prepared, and physically and emotionally capable of defending your property. If in doubt, you should leave early.
